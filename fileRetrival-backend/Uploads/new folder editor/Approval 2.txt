Drones, also known as unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), are aircraft that can fly autonomously or by remote control, and come in various sizes and applications, from hobbyist use to military and commercial applications. 
Here's a more detailed look:
What are they?
Drones are unmanned aircraft that function without an onboard pilot, guided either by remote control or autonomous systems.
Also known as: Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) or Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS). 
Size: They range from small, handheld devices to large, military-grade systems. 
How they fly: Drones can be powered by electric, jet, or combustion motors and are equipped with GPS, radar control, infrared, and high-resolution cameras. 
Uses and Applications:
Military:
Surveillance, reconnaissance, intelligence gathering, and even delivering payloads. 
Commercial:
Photography, videography, delivery services, infrastructure inspections, agriculture (crop monitoring, spraying), and search and rescue operations.
